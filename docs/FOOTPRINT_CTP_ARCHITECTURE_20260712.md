## 0. Prompt Contract

### Goal

Produce the buildable long term architecture for a fully autonomous Footprint engine that understands the whole RedLantern capability estate, operates without routine human approval after commissioning, protects identity and community trust, avoids oversaturation, and produces measurable authority and qualified relationship movement.

### Constraints

1. Claudex remains the studio truth source.
2. The bridge outranks memory snapshots for current state.
3. n8n owns business logic.
4. Supabase stores durable state with RLS.

7. Sentry records failures with context.
8. Official platform access only.
9. No routine approval after commissioning.
10. Hard product, identity, religious, privacy, and platform rules cannot be overridden by model judgment.

### Format

Full CTP, system architecture, data model, decision logic, workflow plan, commissioning gate, metrics, acceptance criteria, definition of done, and final recommendation.

### Failure

The plan fails if it treats LinkedIn as the engine gate, treats every product as equally proven, permits founder promotion through The Lantern, merges Keymon and Kevin, publishes religious claims without scholarly approval, requires routine approval after activation, or cannot explain and reverse every autonomous public action.

Penalties:

1. Saturation risk: up to thirty points.

## 16. Destructive Criticism

### Failure path one: stale truth publishes

Trigger: a narrative memory file says Active while the current bridge says Blocked.

Probability without mitigation: high over time.

Impact: false public claim.

Mitigation: source priority, bridge freshness, evidence expiry, and conflict quarantine.

Residual risk: low when enforced.

### Failure path two: identity authority is invented

Trigger: a model infers Keymon position from commits.

Probability without mitigation: moderate.

Impact: relationship and reputation damage.

Mitigation: identity as a security boundary, default disabled, written consent, claim allowlist, and separate Keymon and Kevin records.

Residual risk: near zero when enforced.

### Failure path three: platform access disappears

Trigger: permission denial, token expiry, policy change, or restriction.

Probability: inevitable across a long enough timeline.

Impact: channel loss.

Mitigation: adapter isolation, owned media core, queue preservation, permission health checks, and no unofficial fallback.

Residual risk: moderate channel impact, low engine impact.

### Failure path four: autonomy learns toward noise

Trigger: impressions and shallow engagement reward broad or sensational content.

Probability without mitigation: high.

Impact: oversaturation and brand drift.

Mitigation: optimize for qualified circle response, relationship movement, proof consumption, opportunities, and trust incidents.

Residual risk: moderate until outcome data matures.

### Failure path five: search automation becomes scaled abuse

Trigger: the engine generates many similar geographic or topic pages.

Probability without mitigation: moderate.

Impact: search penalty and trust damage.

Mitigation: unique evidence requirement, page value test, canonical entity model, fixed page creation budget, and automatic no index for unproven drafts.

Residual risk: low when enforced.
