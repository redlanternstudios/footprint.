## PROMPT CONTRACT

### GOAL

Define everything required to create, own, brand, build, connect, commission, launch, and operate Footprint as a fully autonomous community opportunity network.

### CONSTRAINTS

1. Footprint must help community members first.
2. Full public autonomy begins only after private commissioning passes.
3. LinkedIn is the primary public and intake surface.
4. LinkedIn Page messages require an approved messaging partner.
5. n8n owns business logic.
6. Supabase owns durable data with RLS.
7. Next.js App Router and Tailwind own the interface.
8. SwarmClaw uses its existing organization and separation of powers.
9. Zapier is available but must not become the business logic authority.
10. The Lantern may not carry Rory promotion.
11. Keymon actions require written identity authority.
12. No private submission becomes public without asset specific consent.
13. No unofficial LinkedIn automation.
14. No unnecessary SaaS accounts or duplicate tools.
15. Brand ownership, name clearance, domain, and account recovery must be resolved before public launch.

### FORMAT

Full CTP followed by a complete ordered creation inventory covering ownership, accounts, domains, repository, brand, marketing, social surfaces, infrastructure, integrations, workflows, safety, content, commissioning, launch, operations, acceptance criteria, definition of done, criticism, and KPIs.

### FAILURE

The output fails if it creates public accounts before ownership and name clearance, assumes a personal LinkedIn profile can represent Footprint, assumes Zapier or n8n can read the Page inbox, creates a logo before the name is defensible, scatters the product across repositories, omits consent and removal, or calls a designed system live.

## THREE PASS ANALYSIS

### Pass 1: visible checklist

Create a logo, website, LinkedIn Page, Group, newsletter, social accounts, database, workflows, and launch content.

### Pass 2: challenge

That sequence creates public assets before answering who owns the name, who controls recovery, whether the name can be defended, which account is canonical, and whether the Page inbox can actually connect to the engine.

It also treats branding as graphics. Footprint needs a promise, category, voice, community rules, consent language, selection philosophy, and contributor experience before visual design can be coherent.

### Pass 3: governing truth

Footprint must be created in this order:

1. Ownership and name clearance.
2. Brand foundation.
3. Account and credential architecture.
4. Sanctioned connection proof.
5. Product and data foundation.
6. Complete community workflow.
7. Brand production assets.
8. Founding content and members.
9. Private commissioning.
10. Public autonomous launch.

The order prevents rebranding, lost accounts, broken inbox promises, duplicated systems, and public trust failures.

## TEN LAYER ANALYSIS

### Load bearing decision A: use Footprint as the public name

1. Surface: Footprint is concise, memorable, and connected to lasting work and influence.

2. Root cause: the product helps real work leave a visible professional trace.

3. First order: the name is easy to say and can support contributor storytelling.

4. Second order: Footprint is generic and strongly associated with sustainability, tracking, analytics, identity, and existing software products.

5. Third order: weak distinctiveness can create search competition, domain cost, handle inconsistency, and trademark risk.

6. Upstream dependencies: legal owner, trademark search, domain search, LinkedIn Page search, company registry search, and social handle search.

7. Downstream dependencies: logo, domain, Page URL, Group name, newsletter, repository metadata, marketing kit, press, and partnerships.

8. Failure modes: another relevant mark blocks launch, the desired domain is unavailable, people assume sustainability software, or search results remain ambiguous.

9. Recovery: use a distinctive compound public name while retaining Footprint as a product descriptor. Candidate structure could be Footprint Community, Footprint Works, or a newly cleared coined name. No candidate is approved in this document.

10. Strategic implication: do not purchase a complete visual asset package until clearance passes.

### Load bearing decision B: independent brand endorsed by RedLantern Studios

1. Surface: Footprint has its own public identity with a discreet RedLantern endorsement.

2. Root cause: the community must believe it exists to help members rather than to disguise founder promotion.

3. First order: Footprint can develop its own audience, voice, Page, Group, and newsletter.

4. Second order: RedLantern retains credibility, ownership, operational support, and transparent attribution.

5. Third order: the brand can later support broader stewards and partners without appearing owned by one personality.

6. Upstream dependencies: owner entity, endorsement language, Rory and Keymon roles, conflict policy, and editorial allocation.

7. Downstream dependencies: logo relationship, Page About copy, site footer, press boilerplate, contributor disclosure, and partnership materials.

8. Failure modes: endorsement becomes hidden, RedLantern overwhelms Footprint, or contributors believe commercial selection is neutral when it is not.

9. Recovery: explicit labels, public editorial policy, content allocation, conflict disclosure, and independent fairness audit.

10. Strategic implication: use “Built by RedLantern Studios” quietly in About and footer contexts, not as the main Page identity.

### Load bearing decision C: one product system rather than many tools

1. Surface: reuse existing studio services and add only the missing LinkedIn messaging partner.

2. Root cause: every duplicate scheduler, database, CRM, or repository creates another truth source and failure path.

3. First order: one monorepo and one state model reduce drift.

4. Second order: n8n can coordinate every workflow while SwarmClaw performs bounded reasoning and governance.

5. Third order: audits, correction, consent, and removal can traverse one trace.

6. Upstream dependencies: account inventory, service ownership, environment design, and partner proof.

7. Downstream dependencies: development, security, analytics, cost, recovery, and autonomy.

8. Failure modes: Zapier becomes a second logic engine, Hootsuite and another publisher both post, local n8n sleeps, or separate repositories drift.

9. Recovery: designate one authority per function and disable duplicate side effects.

10. Strategic implication: the only definitely new external category is an approved LinkedIn Page messaging provider.

## CONSTRUCTIVE CRITICISM

The concept has a real emotional and professional value proposition: people can bring unfinished work and receive a useful next step.

The brand becomes weak if it presents itself as another social growth tool. “Community Opportunity Network” and “Share what you are building” keep the focus on people and movement.

The highest leverage brand decision is independent identity with transparent RedLantern endorsement.

## DESTRUCTIVE CRITICISM

### Failure 1: name collision

Trigger: visual production begins before legal and common law clearance.

Impact: forced rename after domains, Page growth, press, and contributor recognition.

Mitigation: Phase 0 clearance gate.

### Failure 2: owner lockout

Trigger: one personal account owns Page, domain, database, and integrations.

Impact: the entire community can become unreachable.

Mitigation: organization ownership, multiple admins, MFA, recovery records, and quarterly access review.

### Failure 3: broken inbox promise

Trigger: Page launches with “message us” before an approved partner and n8n route work.

Impact: slow or lost responses undermine the central promise.

Mitigation: partner proof before launch copy is activated.

### Failure 4: brand becomes Rory content

Trigger: founder material consumes the editorial system.

Impact: contributors feel used.

Mitigation: seventy, twenty, ten allocation and transparent steward role.

### Failure 5: account sprawl

Trigger: each need creates another service and truth source.

Impact: higher cost, duplicate posting, lost credentials, and inconsistent data.

Mitigation: one authority per function and a mandatory integration registry.

### Failure 6: local runtime is treated as production

Trigger: n8n depends on a sleeping laptop.

Impact: missed messages and retries.

Mitigation: always reachable runtime, durable queue, monitoring, and restart proof.

## SINGLE RECOMMENDATION

Run a two part Foundation Gate before any public account creation:

1. Decide the owning legal entity.
2. Complete name, domain, LinkedIn URL, and handle clearance for Footprint.

Once this passes, produce the logo system and create the canonical domain, email, LinkedIn Page, and Group under the correct owner.

Confidence: high.
